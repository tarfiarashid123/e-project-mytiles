Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our tiles

What's the difference between ceramic and porcelain tiles?

Porcelain tiles are denser and more durable than ceramic tiles, making them suitable for high-traffic areas. They're also more water-resistant, while ceramic tiles are generally more affordable and easier to cut for DIY projects.

How do I calculate how many tiles I need for my project?

Measure the length and width of the area to be tiled in feet. Multiply these numbers to get the square footage. Add 10% extra for cuts and waste. Our website has a tile calculator tool to help you with this.

Can tiles be used for both floors and walls?

Many tiles are suitable for both floors and walls, but there are differences. Floor tiles are generally more durable and slip-resistant, while wall tiles may be thinner and have more decorative finishes. Always check the manufacturer's recommendations.

How do I maintain and clean my tiles?

For daily cleaning, sweep or vacuum to remove dirt, then mop with warm water and a pH-neutral cleaner. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface. For grout, use a soft brush and specialized grout cleaner. Seal natural stone tiles annually.
