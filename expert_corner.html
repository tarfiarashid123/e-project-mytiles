Ceramic Tiles

Ceramic tiles are made from clay and other natural materials, then fired in a kiln. They're durable, easy to maintain, and come in a wide variety of colors and patterns. Perfect for moderate-traffic areas.

Porcelain Tiles

Porcelain is a denser, more durable type of ceramic tile. It's highly resistant to moisture, making it ideal for bathrooms, kitchens, and outdoor use. More expensive but longer-lasting than standard ceramic.

Natural Stone

Includes marble, granite, slate, and travertine. Each stone has unique characteristics and requires specific maintenance. Offers a luxurious, high-end look but typically needs sealing to prevent staining.

Small Tiles (1" to 6")

Ideal for mosaic patterns, backsplashes, and small spaces. Provide better traction when used on floors. More grout lines mean more maintenance but can create interesting visual effects.

Medium Tiles (6" to 12")

Versatile size suitable for most residential applications. Good balance between aesthetic appeal and practicality. Works well in bathrooms, kitchens, and living areas.

Large Tiles (12" and up)

Create a seamless look with fewer grout lines. Make small spaces appear larger. Best for open areas; may require more subfloor preparation to prevent cracking.

Daily Cleaning

Sweep or vacuum regularly to remove abrasive dirt. Mop with pH-neutral cleaners specifically formulated for tile. Avoid vinegar or acidic cleaners that can damage grout and some tile surfaces.

Sealing

Most natural stone and some porcelain tiles require periodic sealing. Test if sealing is needed by dropping water on the tile - if it absorbs, it's time to seal. Typically needed every 1-3 years.

Grout Care

Grout is porous and prone to staining. Clean grout lines with a soft brush and grout cleaner. Consider applying a grout sealer after installation and reapply as needed.

Surface Preparation

The substrate must be clean, dry, and level. For floors, deflection must be within acceptable limits to prevent cracking. May require backer board or uncoupling membrane depending on application.

Layout Planning

Dry lay tiles to plan the best layout, minimizing cuts. Start from the center for balanced appearance. Use spacers for consistent grout lines. Consider lighting and sight lines in the room.

Cutting Tiles

Use a wet saw for straight cuts on ceramic and porcelain. For natural stone, a diamond blade is essential. Nippers can be used for small irregular cuts. Always wear safety glasses when cutting tile.
